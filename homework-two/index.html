AMERICAN ALPINE CLUB: World Cup Ice Climbing Comes to the USA

+ First World Cup Ice Climbing Finals ever held in USA
+ Generated the largest live audience for any climbing competition in history
+ Recruited over 60 journalists to attend event
+ Total Unique Articles / TV segments: 150+
+ Equivalent Advertisement Value: $627,092.14
+ Estimated Audience Reach: 370,797,277

In 2019, the American Alpine Club accepted the mammoth responsibility to host the first-ever World Cup Ice Climbing Finals competition to be held in the United States. The event, held in downtown Denver, was also the first time any such ice climbing competition had been held in a major US metropolitan city.

Using PR as the primary vehicle for promotion, Purple Orange organized a promotional campaign starting 9-months in advance of the event. In partnership with Visit Denver, Purple Orange led a massive regional and national media campaign that included The Associated Press, Outside Magazine, Popular Mechanics, Climbing Magazine, Rock + Ice, The Wall Street Journal, SELF, and dozens more. Altogether we brought the sport of ice climbing to living rooms and

newsstands across the United States and drove the largest-ever recorded live attendance for any climbing competition in world history.




The Purple Orange team has done outstanding work producing stories both in our industry and also non-endemic media that we haven’t previously been able to tap into. What really stands out is the time they have spent to get to know the Club and our goals. Purple Orange is not just another public relations firm, they take the time to fully immerse themselves their clients’ world.
— WHITNEY BRADBERRY AAC MARKETING DIRECTOR

CHRIS DICKEY

Founder + Principal
Purple Orange LLC
Chris applies a liberal arts perspective to Purple Orange as a result of a diverse marketing background spanning manufacturing, publishing, and agency work. Early on, Chris served as the first Marketing and Sales Director for then-startup NEMO Equipment, then onto Alpinist Magazine in the role of Marketing and Circulation Director before finally transitioning into agency work with both Stanwood and Partners and Carmichael Lynch.

Chris has helped some of the largest names in the outdoor and active lifestyle industry successfully execute North American brand communication campaigns including Arc’teryx Equipment, Atomic Skis, Jack Links Beef Jerky, and Rollerblade USA.

Chris currently lives in Jackson Hole, WY where he can mountain bike at lunch and climb and ski in the Tetons year round. He is a published writer, award- winning filmmaker, and blogger.
